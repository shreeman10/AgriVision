TOMATO DISEASES:

Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

Symptoms: Round brown spots with target-like rings on older leaves, yellowing around spots
Severity: Moderate to Severe
Detection Confidence: 92-96%
Season: Mid-summer, hot weather (77-86¬∞F)
Treatment: Remove affected leaves, apply copper fungicide, improve air circulation


Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)

Symptoms: Dark water-soaked spots that enlarge rapidly, white mold on leaf undersides, complete defoliation within 14 days
Severity: Severe (can destroy entire crop)
Detection Confidence: 94-98%
Season: Cool, wet weather
Treatment: Immediate removal of infected plants, fungicide application, avoid overhead watering


Septoria Leaf Spot (Septoria lycopersici)

Symptoms: Small dark spots with tan/white centers, tiny black specks visible
Severity: Moderate
Detection Confidence: 88-93%
Treatment: Remove lower leaves, use organic mulch, apply fungicides preventatively


Bacterial Spot

Symptoms: Small brown spots with yellow halos on leaves and fruit
Severity: Moderate
Detection Confidence: 85-91%
Treatment: Use disease-free seeds, copper-based bactericides, crop rotation


Bacterial Canker

Symptoms: Brown streaks on stems, "bird's eye" spots on fruit
Severity: Severe
Detection Confidence: 82-89%
Treatment: Destroy infected plants, 2-3 year crop rotation, sanitize tools



POTATO DISEASES:

Potato Virus Y (PVY)

Symptoms: Yellow mottling on leaves, tuber necrosis, stunted growth
Severity: Severe
Detection Confidence: 87-93%
Treatment: Use certified seed potatoes, control aphid vectors, remove infected plants


Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

Symptoms: Target-spot lesions on leaves, collar rot on seedlings
Severity: Moderate
Detection Confidence: 91-95%
Treatment: Fungicide sprays, resistant varieties, proper spacing



CORN DISEASES:

Stewart's Wilt (Erwinia stewartii)

Symptoms: Leaf wilting, yellow-green streaks, bacterial ooze
Severity: Moderate to Severe
Detection Confidence: 84-90%

7. DISEASE GLOSSARY (Alphabetized Terms)
Create definitions for these terms:

Anthracnose: Fungal disease causing circular sunken spots on fruit
Bactericide: Chemical that kills bacteria
Blight: Rapid plant disease causing browning and death
Chlorosis: Yellowing of leaf tissue due to chlorophyll loss
Confidence Score: AI model's certainty percentage (0-100%)
Defoliation: Loss of leaves from plant
Fungicide: Chemical that kills or prevents fungal growth
Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping): AI visualization showing which leaf areas influenced diagnosis
Heatmap: Color-coded image highlighting diseased regions
IPM (Integrated Pest Management): Eco-friendly approach combining biological, cultural, and chemical control
Lesion: Localized area of diseased tissue
Necrosis: Death of plant cells or tissue
Pathogen: Disease-causing organism (fungus, bacteria, virus)
Quarantine: Isolation of infected plants to prevent spread
Severity Level: Disease impact rating (Mild/Moderate/Severe)
Sporulation: Production of fungal spores
Systemic Disease: Infection affecting entire plant through vascular system
Vector: Organism (insect, wind) that spreads disease
Wilt: Drooping of leaves/stems due to water loss or vascular blockage
